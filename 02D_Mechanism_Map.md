# 02D_Mechanism_Map

## Governing mechanism chain

The chapter reconstructs capacity utilization through the following mechanism chain:

1. **Firm-level contradiction**
   - The firm is not a unified optimizing actor.
   - Management is internally divided.
   - The labor process and valorization process do not coincide smoothly.
   - This generates satisficing behavior, routine inefficiency, and contested coordination.

2. **Decentralized accumulation**
   - Investment decisions are made without ex ante coordination.
   - Firm-level contradictions are generalized through market competition.
   - This produces disproportionality and cumulative disequilibrium.

3. **Utilization as outcome**
   - Capacity utilization is not a given benchmark.
   - It emerges as the historically variable outcome of these contradictions across levels.
   - It expresses the unstable relation between accumulation and productive-capacity use.

4. **Comparative conditioning**
   - In a canonical central case, these contradictions are mediated within a stronger institutional setting.
   - In Chile as a peripheral formation, the same contradictions are additionally conditioned by external constraint, hierarchical state relations, and world-market dependence.

## Minimal diagram

Firm contradiction  
→ decentralized accumulation  
→ disproportionality / disequilibrium  
→ capacity utilization as socially mediated outcome  
→ differentiated behavior across center and periphery

## Design use

This note disciplines the chapter in three ways:

- it prevents utilization from being treated as a purely technical ratio
- it prevents macro dynamics from being detached from firm organization
- it prevents comparison from collapsing into national-container logic

## Scope limit

This mechanism map is sufficient for the present chapter.

It does not yet require:
- full profitability decomposition
- complete dependency-theory deployment
- expanded historical corridor architecture
- full process-tracing dataset construction

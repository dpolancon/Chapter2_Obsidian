# N00: Mechanization Analytical Foundations — Reference Map

## Core Claim
The mechanization problem used in this framework is grounded in a Marxian reinterpretation of induced innovation, where firms choose the rate of mechanization to reduce costs under technological and distributive constraints. 

This note maps the intellectual sources that support that formulation. It does **not** perform derivations or define analytical objects. All analytical operations, object definitions, and transformation rules reside exclusively in the `A`-notes (`A01`–`A04`) and `H`-hinges (`H00`, `H01`).

## 2. Core Mechanization Foundation
**Cajas Guajirro, J. (2024)** *An extended Goodwin model with endogenous technical change and labor supply*  
https://doi.org/10.1016/j.strueco.2024.03.006  
**Role:** Micro-foundation of mechanization as a constrained optimization problem. Mechanization is chosen, not exogenous. Productivity depends nonlinearly on $q$.  
**Function in this framework:** Provides the conceptual basis for treating technical change as a tactical margin choice under distributive conditions.  
**Used in:** `[[A02_Intensive_Accumulation]]`, `[[H01_Cajas_to_Transformation_Elasticity]]`

## 3. Marxian Foundation
**Marx, K. (1867) *Capital, Vol. I*** | **Marx, K. (1885) *Capital, Vol. II*** | **Sweezy, P. (1964) *The Theory of Capitalist Development***  
**Role:** Mechanization as response to wage pressure; machinery as labor-disciplining device; accumulation drives technical change; technical change reshapes class relations.  
**Function in this framework:** Grounds the distributive channel $\pi \downarrow \implies q^* \uparrow$ and frames the optimization as a form of appearance of value under contradiction.  
**Used in:** `[[A02_Intensive_Accumulation]]`, `[[H00_Okishio_Vidal_Decentralized_Accumulation]]`, `[[H01_Cajas_to_Transformation_Elasticity]]`, `[[R05_Frontier_of_Not_Becoming]]`

## 4. Induced Innovation Tradition
**Kennedy, C. (1964)** | **Shah & Desai (1981)** | **Duménil & Lévy (1995, 2003)**  
**Role:** Technical change as cost-minimizing choice under a technological frontier.  
**Limitation:** Focus on capital productivity; do not isolate mechanization ($q$) from aggregate capital.  
**Function in this framework:** Serves as background tradition from which the intensive-margin frontier $a = \Phi(q; K^{NRC})$ is extracted and generalized to two capitals.  
**Used in:** `[[A02_Intensive_Accumulation]]`, `[[A03_Transformation_Elasticity_Two-CapitalCapacityComposition]]`

---

## 5. Kaldor — Productivity & Periphery
**Kaldor, N. (1957)** | **Kaldor, N. (1959) (Chile context)**  
**Role:** Productivity linked to capital deepening; investment depends on structural conditions; capital goods often externally constrained.  
**Function in this framework:** Supports the capacity composition identity and motivates the peripheral extension where $K^{ME}$ accumulation is import-dependent.  
**Used in:** `[[A01_Extensive_Accumulation]]`, `[[A03_Transformation_Elasticity_Two-CapitalCapacityComposition]]`, `[[A04_PeripheralTransformationElasticity]]`

## 6. Basu — Closure & Reproduction
**Basu, D. (2022)** (reproduction schemas) | **Basu, D. (2024)**  
**Role:** Defines normal profitability and accumulation; provides closure conditions (strong labor, weak labor, labor surplus).  
**Function in this framework:** Anchors the normal accumulation corridor for $K^{NRC}$ and justifies the "not becoming" abstraction as a smooth-reproduction benchmark.  
**Empirical Specification Note:** Basu's (2022) dimensional analysis constraint (reference-unit normalization for log-transforms) is intentionally relaxed for empirical specification per advisor guidance. The theoretical closure logic and reproduction schemas remain fully active.  
**Used in:** `[[A01_Extensive_Accumulation]]`, `[[A03_Transformation_Elasticity_Two-CapitalCapacityComposition]]`, `[[R05_Frontier_of_Not_Becoming]]`, `[[R06_Regime_Gating_and_Diagnostic_Ladder]]`

## 7. Marx–Okishio Literature
**Okishio, N. (1961)** | **Morishima, M. (1973)** | **Foley, D. (1986)** | **Roemer, J. (1981)**  
**Role:** Formal theorem on profitability and technical change.  
**Function in this framework:** Reinterpreted as a closure-dependent mechanism rather than a universal law. Regime shifts in $\lambda^{[s]}$ and $\theta^N$ operationalize this reinterpretation.  
**Used in:** `[[H00_Okishio_Vidal_Decentralized_Accumulation]]`, `[[H01_Cajas_to_Transformation_Elasticity]]`, `[[R05_Frontier_of_Not_Becoming]]`, `[[R06_Regime_Gating_and_Diagnostic_Ladder]]`

## 8. Regulationist Tradition
**Aglietta, M. (1979)** | **Boyer, R. (1990)**  
**Role:** Fordism vs post-Fordism; institutional wage stabilization; regime-dependent dynamics.  
**Function in this framework:** Supports the theoretical justification for discrete regime transitions ($s \to s'$) and the diagnostic ladder that gates threshold estimation on extensive-margin closure conditions.  
**Used in:** `[[R06_Regime_Gating_and_Diagnostic_Ladder]]`, `[[R07_Intensive_Margin_Diagnostics]]`

## 9. Peripheral Constraint Literature
**Prebisch, R. (1950)** | **Thirlwall, A. (1979)**  
**Role:** External constraint on accumulation; import dependence of capital goods.  
**Function in this framework:** Justifies the FX-conversion bottleneck $\phi_t$ and the effective profit share squeeze $\pi^{eff} = \pi \cdot \phi_t$ in the periphery.  
**Used in:** `[[A04_PeripheralTransformationElasticity]]`

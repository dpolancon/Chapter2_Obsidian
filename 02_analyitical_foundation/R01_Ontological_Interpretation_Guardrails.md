# R01: Ontological Interpretation Guardrails

## Core Rule
The mechanization frontier, optimal technique $q^*(\pi, s)$, and transformation elasticity $\theta^N(\pi, s)$ are **analytical abstractions of "not becoming"** (smooth reproduction limit). They must never be interpreted as behavioral predictions, efficiency parameters, or equilibrium outcomes.

## 1. What $\theta^N$ Is Not
Do not interpret $\theta^N$ as:
- A technological constant or production-function elasticity
- An optimal allocation of resources
- The outcome of a representative, profit-maximizing firm
- A direct empirical test of the Marx–Okishio theorem

## 2. What $\theta^N$ Is
$\theta^N(\pi, s)$ is a **composition-weighted structural parameter** that maps capital accumulation to productive capacity growth under idealized coordination. It emerges endogenously from the Double Choice of Technique:
- $\theta^{NRC}$: Non-distributive baseline payoff of infrastructure (per [[A01_Extensive_Accumulation]])
- $\theta^{ME}(\pi, s)$: Distributively modulated payoff of machinery (per [[A02_Intensive_Accumulation]])
- $\theta^N$: Aggregate composition-weighted ratio (per [[A03_TransformationElasticity_Two-CapitalCapacityComposition]])

Historical deviations $\theta_t \neq \theta^N$ are not estimation errors. They are the **empirical fingerprints of capitalist "becoming"**: decentralized accumulation, internal firm division (Vidal), utilization gaps ($\mu_t \neq 1$), and peripheral realization bottlenecks ($\pi^{eff} < \pi$).

## 3. Interpretation of Empirical Coefficients
Estimated coefficients ($\beta_1, \beta_2, \beta_3$) do not recover structural elasticities directly. They identify reduced-form traces of distributive conditioning across shifting corridors. 
- $\beta_2 < 0$: Marx-side distributive closure (strong-labor, capital-using mechanization)
- $\beta_2 > 0$: Okishio-side closure (weak-labor, productivity-enhancing mechanization)
- $\beta_2$ sign instability: Signals regime transition or peripheral FX-realization gate activation (per [[A04_PeripheralTransformationElasticity]])

## 4. Guardrail Language
**Do not write:** "$\theta$ measures technological efficiency" or "This tests Marx–Okishio directly."
**Write instead:** "$\theta$ measures the historically realized transformation of accumulation into productive capacity under decentralized accumulation and internally contradictory firm behavior. The estimated coefficients operationalize Marx–Okishio closure logic at the macro-regime level."

## Locked Formulation
All transformation objects are historically contingent outcomes, not equilibrium parameters. The frontier provides the geometric benchmark; historical realization traces the scars of market anarchy, internal division, and external constraint. Interpretation must remain strictly subordinate to the Okishio–Vidal ontology (per [[H00_Okishio_Vidal_Decentralized_Accumulation]]) and the constitutional rule against residual-defined capacity.

# Interpretation of θ as a contradictory object

## Core rule

The transformation elasticity:

$$
\theta_t^N
=
1+\frac{a_t-q_t}{g_{K,t}^N}
$$

must not be interpreted as an optimal or equilibrium outcome.

It is:

> a historically realized outcome of decentralized accumulation and internally contradictory firm behavior.

---

## 1. What θ is not

Do not interpret \( \theta_t \) as:

- an efficiency parameter  
- a production-function elasticity  
- a technological constant  
- the result of optimal allocation  
- the outcome of a representative firm  

---

## 2. Ontological grounding

From Okishio:

- accumulation is decentralized  
- coordination is ex post  
- disequilibrium is structural  

:contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}  

From the mechanism chain:

- disproportionality generates idle capacity  
- idle capacity reflects coordination failure  

:contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}  

From the unified-firm gap:

- firms are not coherent decision units  
- market anarchy is internalized, not resolved  

:contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}  

---

## 3. Vidal articulation

The firm is internally divided:

- labor process vs control  
- plant vs corporate strategy  
- operational vs financial objectives  
- competing capacity conventions  

Therefore:

> there is no single optimization problem generating \(q_t\)

---

## 4. Consequence for mechanization

Mechanization:

$$
q_t
$$

is not:

> an optimal choice

It is:

> a realized outcome of contradictory internal responses to market pressure  

This implies:

- over-mechanization may coexist with low profitability  
- under-mechanization may coexist with high demand  
- timing of mechanization is inconsistent  
- sectoral and firm-level divergence is structural  

---

## 5. Consequence for productivity

Labor productivity:

$$
a_t
$$

is not a smooth function of mechanization.

It reflects:

- uneven implementation of technology  
- organizational frictions  
- labor resistance  
- scale-specific constraints  

Therefore:

$$
a_t - q_t
$$

is:

> a residual of contradiction, not a clean technical relation  

---

## 6. Consequence for accumulation

The denominator:

$$
g_{K,t}^N = \chi_t^N r_t^N - \delta_t
$$

is also not mechanically determined.

It reflects:

- financial constraints  
- governance conflict  
- external conditions (in the periphery)  
- incomplete recapitalization  

---

## 7. Interpretation of θ

Thus:

$$
\theta_t^N
$$

captures:

- the capacity payoff of accumulation  
- under conditions of contradiction  
- without assuming coordination  

It is:

> a macro-level outcome of micro-level disunity  

---

## 8. Empirical implication

The estimated:

$$
\theta(\omega_t) = \beta_1 + \beta_2 \omega_t
$$

must be read as:

- a reduced-form outcome  
- not a structural optimum  

So:

- \(\beta_2 < 0\) does not mean “inefficiency”  
- \(\beta_2 > 0\) does not mean “optimal response”  

It means:

> different regime-specific configurations of contradiction  

---

## 9. Relation to Cajas hinge

Cajas provides:

$$
q^*(\omega)
$$

as a coherent benchmark.

This framework interprets it as:

> a limiting case of coordinated behavior  

Actual outcomes:

$$
q_t \neq q^*(\omega)
$$

because:

- firms are internally divided  
- coordination is incomplete  

---

## 10. Relation to threshold model

Threshold estimation:

$$
\theta =
\begin{cases}
\theta_L \\
\theta_H
\end{cases}
$$

does not identify:

> different equilibria  

It identifies:

> different regimes of contradiction  

---

## 11. Guardrail

Do not write:

> θ measures technological efficiency  
> θ measures optimal transformation  
> θ reflects production-function elasticity  

Write instead:

> θ measures the historically realized transformation of accumulation into productive capacity under decentralized accumulation and internally contradictory firm behavior  

---

## 12. Final lock

θ is a contradictory object.

It summarizes:

- decentralized accumulation (Okishio)  
- internal firm fracture (Vidal)  
- uneven mechanization  
- constrained recapitalization  

It is not an equilibrium object.

It is a historically contingent outcome.

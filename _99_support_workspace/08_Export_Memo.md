# Export memo

This Obsidian bundle develops the theoretical core of a critique of the firm-level normal degree of capacity utilization. Its central claim is that the normal degree depends on a hidden unified-firm assumption. Against that assumption, the bundle reconstructs the capitalist firm as a contradictory social organization divided across labor process, plant-level operations, managerial hierarchy, governance, finance, and measurement conventions.

The bundle’s positive concept is **contradictory utilization configurations**   [[Artifacts/01_contradictory_utilization_configurations/01_Theoretical_Core_Notebook|01_Theoretical_Core_Notebook]]

This concept names the firm-level settlement through which firms coordinate labor control, cognitive mobilization, throughput, learning, tactical slack, strategic pressure, and capacity measurement.

 [[Artifacts/01_contradictory_utilization_configurations/01_Theoretical_Core_Notebook|01_Theoretical_Core_Notebook]]
**Utilization regimes** are reserved for the higher-order historical and institutional layer that organizes, stabilizes, and distributes those configurations across firms, sectors, and periods.

The Okishio satellite bundle supplies the macro-theoretical bridge. It explains why the unified-firm abstraction matters for accumulation theory and why the firm-level mediation must be reconstructed rather than assumed.

Use `01_Theoretical_Core_Notebook.md` as the main notebook. Use the Okishio satellite only as bridge support. Do not promote it into a sibling root.

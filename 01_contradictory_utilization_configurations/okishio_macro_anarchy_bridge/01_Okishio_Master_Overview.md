# Okishio Master Overview

Okishio’s relevance for the utilization project lies in his reconstruction of capitalist accumulation as a decentralized and market-mediated process that cannot guarantee smooth coordination. Investment decisions are privately made, socially interdependent, and only validated after the fact through market outcomes. This produces a structural problem of disproportionality, disequilibrium, idle capacity, and crisis.

The useful point is not simply that capitalism is unstable. The useful point is that instability follows from a coordination form. Firms act under decentralized constraints and respond to profitability, demand, costs, and prices, but their aggregate interaction does not produce automatic coherence.

For the utilization-configuration bundle, this matters because the firm cannot be treated as a site where market anarchy is simply overcome. The firm internalizes market anarchy through constrained planning. That planning is not unified; it is fractured across labor process, plant-level operations, governance conflict, and capacity conventions.

# Core mechanism

decentralized accumulation  
→ disproportionality  
→ macro disequilibrium  
→ idle capacity  
→ crisis tendencies

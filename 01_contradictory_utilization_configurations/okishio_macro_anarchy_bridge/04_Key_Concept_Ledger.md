# Key Concept Ledger

| Concept | Working definition | Use in Chapter 2 |
|---|---|---|
| [[market anarchy]] | Coordination failure generated by decentralized, private accumulation and ex post market validation. | Macro-theoretical backdrop for the fractured firm. |
| [[decentralized accumulation]] | Investment decisions made by separate capitals without conscious social coordination. | Starting point for disproportionality and crisis. |
| [[macro disequilibrium]] | Aggregate instability generated by uncoordinated decisions across sectors and time. | Shows why equilibrium utilization cannot be presumed. |
| [[idle capacity]] | Unused productive capacity emerging from demand, disproportionality, and coordination failure. | Bridge to capacity utilization theory. |
| [[unified firm abstraction]] | The treatment of firms as coherent agents responding to macro signals. | Bridge concept to the utilization-configuration critique. |
| [[contradictory utilization configurations]] | Firm-level organizational settlements under contradiction. | Landing object of the main bundle. |
| [[utilization regimes]] | Higher-order historical patterns organizing configurations. | Regime layer above firm configurations. |

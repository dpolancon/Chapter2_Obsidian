# Okishio and the unified-firm gap

Okishio is useful because he clarifies the macro-level problem of capitalist coordination. Accumulation is decentralized, privately organized, and mediated by market signals that do not guarantee smooth reproduction. The result is disproportionality, disequilibrium, idle capacity, and recurrent crisis tendencies.

That strength also defines the limit that matters for the utilization paper. Okishio’s firm is generally treated as a unified agent responding to profitability, demand, and market disequilibrium. This abstraction is powerful for explaining aggregate instability, but it leaves the internal organization of the firm underdeveloped.

The bridge to the utilization-configuration bundle is precise. Okishio explains why capitalist accumulation generates market anarchy at the macro level. The utilization-configuration framework explains how that anarchy is internalized inside the firm through divided management, scalar disunity, governance conflict, and contested capacity conventions.

The firm does not overcome market anarchy. It internalizes it through constrained and contradictory planning.

# Accumulation and Disequilibrium Reconstruction

## Mechanism chain

1. Accumulation is decentralized.
2. Investment decisions are privately organized.
3. Market signals coordinate only after the fact.
4. Sectoral and temporal disproportionalities emerge.
5. Disequilibrium appears through unsold output, price pressure, unused capacity, and crisis.
6. Idle capacity is not an accidental residue; it is a symptom of coordination failure.

## Bridge trigger

This mechanism often presumes firms respond as unified agents.

That presumption is precisely the bridge to the main bundle. If the firm is internally divided, then macro disequilibrium is not merely transmitted to firms; it is internalized through contradictory planning, labor control, managerial hierarchy, governance pressure, and capacity measurement.

## Use

Use this note when connecting macro accumulation theory to the critique of the firm-level normal degree of capacity utilization.

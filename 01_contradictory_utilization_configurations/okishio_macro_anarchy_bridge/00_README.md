# Okishio Macro-Anarchy Bridge Bundle

## Bundle status

Linked satellite bundle inside:

`01_contradictory_utilization_configurations/linked_bundles/okishio_macro_anarchy_bridge/`

This bundle does not replace the main utilization-configuration bundle. It supplies the macro-theoretical bridge that explains why the firm-level critique is necessary.

## Core function

Okishio explains capitalist instability at the level of decentralized accumulation, market anarchy, disproportionality, disequilibrium, idle capacity, and crisis.

The utilization-configuration framework explains what Okishio largely leaves abstract: how this macro anarchy is internalized inside the firm through divided management, scalar disunity, governance conflict, and contested capacity conventions.

## Main bridge claim

Okishio clarifies why capitalist accumulation cannot coordinate itself through decentralized investment and market mediation. But this account usually treats firms as unified agents responding to profitability, demand, and disequilibrium. The utilization-configuration framework fills that gap by theorizing the firm as internally contradictory rather than as a coherent planning unit.

## One-line lock

Okishio explains why capitalism cannot coordinate itself; contradictory utilization configurations explain why firms cannot internally resolve that lack of coordination.

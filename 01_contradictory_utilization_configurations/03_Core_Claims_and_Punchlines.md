# Core claims

## Main claim

A [[firm-level normal degree of capacity utilization]] cannot be treated as a coherent whole-firm benchmark because the capitalist firm is internally divided across labor-process organization, scalar managerial tiers, governance pressures, and contested capacity conventions.

## Stronger version

What appears in theory as a firm-wide utilization norm is, in practice, a contradictory settlement among competing organizational imperatives: discipline versus empowerment, manualized effort versus cognitive labor extraction, throughput versus learning, plant-level constraints versus corporate finance, and engineering capacity versus managerial reporting.

## Positive concept

[[contradictory utilization configurations]]

This concept replaces the idea of a single firm-wide normal degree with historically and organizationally specific settlements among labor control, production routines, managerial hierarchy, governance pressure, demand conditions, and capacity conventions.

## Regime/configuration distinction

- [[contradictory utilization configurations]]: firm-level organizational settlements.
- [[utilization regimes]]: higher-order historical and institutional patterns that organize, stabilize, and distribute configurations across firms, sectors, and periods.

## Okishio bridge

Okishio supplies the macro-theoretical backdrop: capitalist accumulation is decentralized, market-mediated, and crisis-prone. The firm appears as a profit-seeking unit inside this macro logic, but its internal organization remains underdeveloped.

The contradictory-utilization-configuration framework fills that gap. It explains how macro market anarchy is internalized inside the firm through divided management, scalar disunity, governance conflict, and contested capacity conventions.

The firm does not overcome market anarchy. It internalizes it through constrained and contradictory planning.

## Final locked punchline

The firm-level normal degree fails not because firms lack routines, targets, or planning, but because those routines, targets, and plans are produced through [[contradictory utilization configurations]]. [[Utilization regimes]] name the broader historical layer that organizes and distributes these configurations across firms, sectors, and periods.

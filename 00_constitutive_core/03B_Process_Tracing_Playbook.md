# 03B_Process_Tracing_Playbook

## Definition

Process tracing is used here as a logic of explanation. It links observed capacity-utilization patterns to the sequence of mechanisms through which they are produced.

## Function

In this chapter, process tracing does not build a full qualitative dataset or a complete historical reconstruction. Its function is narrower: to interpret the estimated capacity-utilization patterns by reconstructing the causal sequence linking productive-capacity formation, realization, profitability, recapitalization, and growth.

## Governing distinction

The playbook distinguishes two linked but non-identical corridors.

### 1. Productive-capacity corridor

**θ → μ → r**

- **θ**: elasticity of productive capacities with respect to productive capital accumulation
- **μ**: utilization / realization
- **r**: profit rate

This corridor identifies the regime and its dominant contradiction.

### 2. Recapitalization corridor

**χ → k → g**

- **χ**: recapitalization dynamics
- **k**: capital accumulation in motion
- **g**: economic growth

This corridor identifies whether money capital is actually reinserted into the circuit and translated into renewed accumulation and growth.

## Regime rule

The governing classification is determined by the elasticity of productive capacities with respect to productive capital accumulation.

- When **θ > 1**, capitalist reproduction tends toward overaccumulation of productive capacities. A monotonic convergent path above the normal/full benchmark is feasible only if realization is maintained; otherwise the path diverges.

- When **θ < 1**, capitalist reproduction tends toward overaccumulation driven by excess accumulation demand, because productive capital accumulation outpaces the growth of productive capacities.

The benchmark path is subordinate to the regime classification: it indicates formal feasibility, while the regime logic identifies the dominant contradiction and the conditions under which the path is sustained or breaks down.

## Measurement rule

The productive-capacity corridor is defined in terms of **gross productive capital accumulation building productive capacities**, not book value or financial valuation.

The recapitalization corridor is defined in terms of **net capital accumulation in current prices**, that is, the injection of money capital into the circuit.

## Comparative use

The comparison between the canonical central case and Chile as a peripheral formation does not proceed by treating cases as self-contained containers. It proceeds by tracing how the articulation between the two corridors differs across unequal positions in the world market within a hierarchically organized world of states.

## Use in this chapter

This playbook is used to:

- interpret estimated capacity-utilization patterns
- connect those patterns to regime logic
- distinguish productive-capacity dynamics from recapitalization dynamics
- organize comparison between center and periphery

## Exclusions

This note does not authorize:

- full profitability decomposition
- full dependency-theory deployment
- complete world-historical reconstruction
- extended qualitative datasets
- Boolean or machine-learning architecture

Those belong to the programmatic horizon unless directly required by the present chapter.

## Working formula

**θ and μ condition r.  
χ determines whether profitability is translated into renewed accumulation and growth.**

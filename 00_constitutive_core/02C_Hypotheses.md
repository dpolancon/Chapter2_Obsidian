# 02C_Hypotheses

## H1 — Theoretical hypothesis

Capacity utilization cannot be treated as a given macroeconomic ratio. It must be reconstructed as a socially mediated outcome of capitalist reproduction, emerging from the articulation between decentralized accumulation (Okishio) and the internally divided organization of the firm (Vidal).

## H2 — Comparative hypothesis

Once reconstructed, capacity utilization will not exhibit the same empirical behavior across structurally unequal positions. Its dynamics will differ between a canonical central case and Chile as a peripheral formation, reflecting external constraint and hierarchical position within the world market.

## Scope note

These hypotheses are not designed to test a closed model. They guide interpretation and comparison of the estimated utilization series. Their role is to discipline the reconstruction of the object, not to exhaustively validate a general theory of crisis.

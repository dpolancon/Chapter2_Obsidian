# Paper-facing Chapter 2 — section map

## Section 1 — The unresolved problem

### Burden

Establish that capacity utilization remains unresolved when treated as:

- a given macroeconomic ratio,
- an inherited benchmark,
- or a residual left after fitting output to capital.

### What the section must do

Open with the problem itself, not with chapter management. Show that the real issue is not “how to measure utilization” in a narrow technical sense, but how to identify the transformation of accumulation into productive capacity under capitalist disequilibrium.

### What must appear

- insufficiency of inherited treatments
- smooth reproduction as formal reference, not realized tendency
- disequilibrium as constitutive, not accidental
- need for structural reconstruction

### What must not appear

- long literature catalogues
- full center–periphery discussion yet
- estimator debates
- detailed methodological caveats

### Closing line burden

This section should end by saying, in substance:  
**the utilization problem cannot be resolved at the level of the ratio alone; it must be reconstructed through the relation that governs productive-capacity formation.**

---

## Section 2 — The reconstruction across levels

### Burden

Build the theoretical reconstruction by making each author carry one necessary layer.

### Internal sequence

Shaikh → unresolved problem  
Okishio → decentralized accumulation, disproportionality, disequilibrium  
Vidal → internally divided firm, meso mediation

### What the section must do

Show that the unresolved status of utilization is not an isolated empirical inconvenience. It comes from the fact that capitalist reproduction does not proceed through smooth proportionality. The benchmark remains necessary as formal feasibility, but the actual movement is governed by internal division and disproportionality.

### What must appear

- smooth reproduction as benchmark only
- capitalist reproduction as non-realization of that benchmark
- firm-level contradiction scaling into macro disequilibrium
- mechanism, not author summary

### What must not appear

- literature review tone
- too much signposting of architecture
- all debates at once

### Closing line burden

This section should end by opening the hinge object:  
**the relevant issue is therefore the relation through which accumulation becomes productive capacity.**

---

## Section 3 — The hinge object: θ\thetaθ

### Burden

Constitute θ\thetaθ as the key object of endogenization and empirical identification.

### What the section must do

This is the center of the chapter. It must establish that:

- θ\thetaθ governs the transformation of accumulation into productive capacity
- θ\thetaθ is mediated by distribution
- μ\muμ can only be reconstructed after θ\thetaθ is identified

### What must appear

- the transformation elasticity as the hinge object
- distribution as mediation, not external control
- the chain:
    
    ω→θ→Yp→μ\omega \rightarrow \theta \rightarrow Y^p \rightarrow \muω→θ→Yp→μ
- μ\muμ is not a residual

### What must not appear

- long estimator discussion
- too much normalization detail
- overloaded comparative material

### Closing line burden

End with the sharp claim:  
**capacity utilization is not what remains after trend estimation; it is what becomes identifiable once the transformation elasticity has been structurally recovered.**

---

## Section 4 — Empirical reconstruction logic

### Burden

Translate the theoretical object into an empirical logic of identification.

### What the section must do

Distinguish:

- observed objects: YYY, KKK, ω\omegaω
- recovered objects: θ\thetaθ, YpY^pYp, μ\muμ

Then show the empirical sequence:

long-run relation→θ^→Y^p→μ^\text{long-run relation} \rightarrow \hat{\theta} \rightarrow \hat{Y}^p \rightarrow \hat{\mu}long-run relation→θ^→Y^p→μ^​

Explain that pinch-year normalization is required for levels, but does not define the object.

### What must appear

- observed vs recovered distinction
- structural reconstruction of μ\muμ
- pinch-year normalization as level anchor only
- super-consistency distinction only if needed very briefly

### What must not appear

- full estimator-family fight
- threshold-model critique in full
- code or workflow talk

### Closing line burden

This section should end with:  
**empirical identification therefore targets the transformation relation directly; utilization is reconstructed only downstream of it.**

---

## Section 5 — Comparative determination: center and periphery

### Burden

Show how the same object is differently determined across center and periphery.

### What the section must do

Block the norm/deviation reading. The comparison is not:

- center realizes the benchmark
- periphery fails

It is:

- same structural object
- different mediations of the transformation relation

### What must appear

- U.S. as cleaner long-run transformation relation
- Chile as recursive, externally mediated, composition-sensitive
- same identification logic, different determination of θ\thetaθ
- external constraint as constitutive in the periphery

### What must not appear

- full dependency reconstruction
- Chapter 3 historical corridor material in full
- long external-constraint digression

### Closing line burden

End with:  
**the difference between center and periphery lies not in whether utilization exists as an object, but in how the transformation of accumulation into productive capacity is structurally mediated.**

---

## Section 6 — Bounded conclusion

### Burden

Restate the reconstruction and stop cleanly.

### What the section must do

Narrow the contribution. No manifesto. No expansion into the whole dissertation.

### What must appear

- what was reconstructed
- what object was clarified
- what comparison was made possible
- what remains outside scope

### What must not appear

- new theory
- full crisis theory
- full profitability agenda
- full dependency deployment

### Closing line burden

Use a narrow ending:  
**The chapter reconstructs capacity utilization as a structurally mediated object and identifies the transformation elasticity as the hinge of its empirical recovery across center and periphery, while leaving the broader crisis and profitability architecture for subsequent work.**

# What gets cut or subordinated

These belong in notes, appendices, later chapters, or highly compressed mentions only:

- full profitability agenda
- full dependency-theory reconstruction
- extended historiography unless directly functional
- full DOLS/FM-OLS/IM-OLS/FGLS debates
- threshold-model technical details
- code workflow and repo exposition
- long excursus on WBOP architecture
- repeated clarifications of what the chapter is and is not doing

# Governing register

This is the tone lock for the whole paper-facing chapter:

- direct
- contradiction-driven
- mechanism-first
- historically aware
- adversarial where needed
- bounded in claim
- low on administrative self-commentary

# One-sentence constitutional test

If any section cannot be defended as serving this sentence, it should be cut or subordinated:

**Chapter 2 reconstructs capacity utilization as a structurally mediated object by identifying the transformation elasticity through which distribution conditions the conversion of accumulation into productive capacity under capitalist disequilibrium, and by comparing how that same problem is differently determined in the center and the periphery.**

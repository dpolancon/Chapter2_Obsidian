# Composition of accumulation and transformation

## Core claim

Productive capacity depends on the composition of accumulation, with asymmetric roles for capital components.

---

## 1. Accumulation decomposition

$$
I_t^A = I_t^M + I_t^O
$$

Define machinery share:

$$
\varphi_t = \frac{I_t^M}{I_t^A}
$$

---

## 2. Capital structure

$$
K_t = K_t^M + K_t^O
$$

Define:

$$
s_t = \frac{K_t^M}{K_t}
$$

---

## 3. Capacity structure

$$
Y_t^p = \theta_t^M K_t^M + \theta^O K_t^O
$$

with:

- $\theta_t^M$ → machinery-specific transformation  
- $\theta^O$ → infrastructure contribution (constant or slow-moving)  

---

## 4. Functional roles

- $K_t^M$:
  - drives mechanization  
  - determines $q_t$  
  - enters productivity through $a(q_t, K_t^O)$  

- $K_t^O$:
  - contributes directly to capacity  
  - conditions productivity  
  - does not determine mechanization  

---

## 5. Aggregate representation

$$
Y_t^p = K_t \left[ s_t \theta_t^M + (1 - s_t)\theta^O \right]
$$

---

## 6. Link to transformation

Substitute:

$$
\theta_t^M = 1 + \frac{a_t - q_t}{g_{K,t}^N}
$$

Thus:

- transformation depends on:
  - mechanization  
  - productivity  
  - composition  
  - normal accumulation benchmark  

---
## Notation rule for θ

Unless explicitly indexed, $$\theta$$ denotes the aggregate transformation coefficient:

$$
\theta \equiv \theta^{tot}
$$

The machinery-specific transformation is denoted:

$$
\theta^M
$$

Indices are used only when decomposition across capital components is required.

## Locked formulation

All capital contributes to productive capacity, but only machinery determines the transformation elasticity through its effect on mechanization and productivity. Infrastructure contributes directly to capacity and conditions productivity, without determining the mechanization response. The transformation is evaluated relative to the normal accumulation path.

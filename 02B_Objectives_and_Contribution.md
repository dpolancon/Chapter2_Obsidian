# 02B_Objectives_and_Contribution

## Objective

To reconstruct the theory of capacity utilization by treating it as a socially mediated outcome of capitalist reproduction rather than as a given macroeconomic ratio.

## Specific objectives

1. To restate Shaikh’s capacity-utilization problem as an unresolved measurement and theoretical problem.
2. To rework that problem through Okishio’s macroeconomic theory of decentralized accumulation, disproportionality, and crisis.
3. To articulate that reconstruction through Vidal’s theory of the internally divided firm.
4. To examine how the reconstructed object appears in a canonical central case and in Chile as a peripheral formation.

## Contribution

The chapter contributes a macro-meso reconstruction of capacity utilization. It takes Shaikh’s unresolved measurement problem as a starting point, reworks it through Okishio’s macroeconomic dynamics, and articulates that reconstruction through Vidal’s theory of the firm. In this way, capacity utilization is respecified as a socially and institutionally mediated outcome rather than as a purely technical ratio or inherited benchmark.

## Bounded contribution

The chapter does not provide a full theory of crisis, a complete dependency-theory reconstruction, or a full deployment of the profitability agenda. Its contribution is narrower: to rebuild the capacity-utilization problem across levels and to show its comparative relevance through a bounded center–periphery comparison.
